About

As the Senior Deputy Director General for Public Health and Professional Standards at Kenya's Ministry of Health, I lead strategic initiatives to strengthen our national healthcare system and elevate professional standards across the sector. With over two decades of experience in public health leadership, I've spearheaded numerous successful programs in disease control and health system strengthening. My expertise in Vector-Borne and Neglected Tropical Diseases has been instrumental in developing comprehensive strategies for disease elimination and control. Through evidence-based approaches and strong partnerships, we continue to make significant progress in improving healthcare accessibility and quality for all Kenyans.

Served as Head of Division | Vector-Borne and Neglected Tropical Diseases

A consultant clinical pharmacist and public health expert leading national efforts to eliminate neglected tropical diseases in Kenya.

  • Position: Senior Deputy Director Ministry of Health
  • Experience: 15+ years in Public Health
  • Expertise: Clinical Pharmacy, NTDs
  • Education: MPharm, Clinical Pharmacy
  • Leadership: Chair, African NTD Program Managers
  • Focus: Disease Elimination Strategies

Having served as the Head of the Division of Vector-Borne and Neglected Tropical Diseases at Kenya's Ministry of Health, I led national efforts to eliminate debilitating diseases that affect millions of Kenyans. I serve as the chairperson of the African Neglected Tropical Diseases Program Managers' Community of Practice and the Leishmaniasis East Africa Platform. My work focused on developing evidence-based strategies, implementing targeted interventions, and building sustainable epidemiological capacity to combat neglected tropical diseases. Through partnerships and innovative approaches, we made significant strides in disease surveillance, prevention, and elimination across Kenya.

Fighting Schistosomiasis: A Focus on Preschool-Aged Children

Schistosomiasis is one of the neglected tropical diseases. While it can be safely and effectively treated in school-aged children and adults, an estimated 50 million preschool-aged children have been left untreated in public health programs primarily due to the lack of an appropriate child-friendly medication.

GHIT Fund has supported and invested in the Pediatric Praziquantel Consortium's development of a new treatment for preschool-aged children since 2013.

We will be releasing videos of interviews with Pediatric Praziquantel Consortium's partners, Merck and Astellas Pharma, and then with Ministries of Health from African countries about the fight against schistosomiasis.

We interviewed Dr. Sultani Matendechero, Deputy Director General for Health in Kenya, about the current situation and future issues of #schistosomiasis, which has a serious impact on preschool children.
